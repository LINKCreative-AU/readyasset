How does boat finance work?

Updated 8 July 2026

Boat finance is a secured marine loan: you apply once, we take your scenario to 45+ lenders — including marine specialists — and you get your best options from the panel, usually with same-day pre-approval and no credit score impact at the quote stage. The boat, motor and trailer package can all be financed together as one secured asset.

New or used, dealer or private sale, our panel covers everything from tinnies and trailer boats through to cruisers. Marine-specialist lenders often price sharper than a general bank on watercraft because they understand the asset and its resale market.

Terms typically run 1 to 7 years with optional deposits and balloon payments. For private sales, the lender verifies the boat and any encumbrances, then pays the seller directly — we manage that paperwork end to end.

How your boat loan can be shaped.

The structure moves the repayment as much as the rate does. We model the options; you pick what fits.

Secured marine loan

The boat (with motor and trailer) secures the loan, keeping rates well under unsecured borrowing. The package settles as one asset.

Terms from 1–7 years

Match the term to how long you’ll keep the boat. Shorter saves interest; longer keeps the weekly number easy.

Optional balloon payment

Popular on larger boats — a balloon holds part of the price to the end of the term so the weekly repayment stays manageable.

Deposit optional

Finance the full package price if approved, or put down a deposit or trade to shrink the loan.

Boat finance questions, answered.

The things people ask us every week about boat finance.

Can I finance the boat, motor and trailer together?

Yes — a package of hull, outboard and trailer is the standard way boat loans are written. Electronics and safety gear included in the purchase can often be financed too.

Do lenders finance used and private-sale boats?

Yes. Used boats and private sales are routine: the lender runs PPSR and encumbrance checks on the vessel and pays the seller at settlement. Age and value limits vary by lender, which is where a 45+ panel helps.

Will a boat finance quote impact my credit score?

No. The quick quote is assessed against the lender panel without any credit enquiry. Your credit file is only checked when you proceed with a chosen lender.

How long can a boat loan run?

Typically 1 to 7 years. Larger amounts are often structured with a balloon payment to keep weekly repayments manageable — we’ll model both so you can see the difference before you decide.

Is boat finance more expensive than a car loan?

Marine rates are usually a touch above car rates but well below unsecured lending, because the loan is secured against the boat. Where your rate lands depends on your profile and the asset — the panel competes, and you see your options with no obligation.

Can I get boat finance with a casual or self-employed income?

Yes — our panel includes lenders comfortable with self-employed, contractor and casual income, including low-doc style assessments for ABN holders. The quick quote will show which lenders suit your situation.

Do I need insurance to finance a boat?

Yes — comprehensive marine insurance is required while the loan runs. Premiums vary with the boat and where you use it; factor it into the weekly cost when you’re comparing options.

Does it matter if the boat is moored or trailered?

It can. Trailered boats are straightforward for most lenders; larger moored vessels involve different valuations and sometimes marine surveys. Tell us how you’ll keep the boat and we’ll match lenders comfortable with it.

What affects my boat loan rate?

Your credit profile, the boat’s age, value and type, the term, and deposit. Marine specialists often out-price generalist banks on watercraft — the panel comparison finds out quickly.
