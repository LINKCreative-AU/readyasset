How does car finance work?

Updated 8 July 2026

Car finance works like this: you apply once, we take your scenario to a panel of 45+ banks, fintechs and specialist lenders, they compete for your business, and we come back — usually the same day — with your best options from the panel and a pre-approval. The initial quote takes about a minute and doesn't touch your credit file, so you can find out where you stand before you commit to anything.

Most car loans are secured against the vehicle itself, which is why they typically price lower than unsecured borrowing. You can finance a brand-new car from a dealer, a used car, or a private sale — the paperwork differs slightly, but the process on your side is the same quick quote.

Terms usually run from 1 to 7 years, with optional deposits, trade-ins and balloon payments to shape the repayment to your budget. Once you pick a lender, we handle the paperwork through to settlement and the money goes straight to the seller.

How your car loan can be shaped.

The structure moves the repayment as much as the rate does. We model the options; you pick what fits.

Secured against the car

The vehicle is the security, which is why car loan rates sit well below unsecured lending. The car is yours to drive from day one.

Terms from 1–7 years

Shorter terms cost less in total interest; longer terms lower the weekly repayment. We model both so you choose with the numbers in front of you.

Optional balloon payment

Park a portion of the price at the end of the loan to cut the weekly repayment — then pay it out, refinance it, or trade the car.

Deposit optional

Many lenders on the panel offer 100% finance for approved applicants. A deposit or trade-in simply lowers the amount financed.

Car finance questions, answered.

The things people ask us every week about car finance.

Will getting a car finance quote affect my credit score?

No. The initial quick quote is an assessment against our lender panel, not a credit application — nothing is recorded on your credit file. A credit check only happens later, once you choose a lender and formally apply.

How fast can I get approved for a car loan?

Pre-approval is typically same-day, and often within a few hours during business hours. Full approval and settlement usually follow within 24–48 hours once your documents are in, depending on the lender.

Can I finance a private sale or only dealer cars?

Both. Private-sale car loans are common — the lender simply runs extra checks on the vehicle (PPSR, payout of any existing finance) before paying the seller directly. We handle those checks as part of the process.

Do I need a deposit for car finance?

Usually no. Many lenders on our panel offer 100% finance on cars, subject to their assessment. A deposit or trade-in lowers the amount financed and the repayments, but it isn’t a requirement for most approved applicants.

Can I get car finance with a poor credit history?

Often, yes. Our panel includes specialist lenders who look at your current situation rather than just your file. Rates are tiered to risk, so you’ll see realistic options rather than a flat no — with no credit score impact to find out.

What is a balloon payment on a car loan?

A balloon is a lump sum left owing at the end of the term. It lowers your weekly repayment along the way, and at the end you can pay it out, refinance it, or sell/trade the car to cover it. We’ll model it both ways so you can compare.

Should I take dealer finance or arrange my own?

It’s worth comparing. A dealership quotes from the arrangements it has; we run your scenario across 45+ lenders and they compete on your rate. Having your own pre-approval in hand also makes you a cash buyer at the negotiating table.

What affects my car loan interest rate?

Mainly your credit history, income stability, whether you own property, the age of the car, the loan term, and the deposit. Lenders weigh these differently — which is exactly why running one scenario across the whole panel beats asking one bank.

Can I refinance my existing car loan?

Yes. If your rate was set when your credit profile was weaker, or dealer finance was taken in a hurry, refinancing can lower the repayment or the term. We run the payout figure against the panel and show you whether switching actually saves money.

Is the rate different for new and used cars?

Usually slightly. New and near-new cars attract the sharpest pricing; older cars price a little higher and some lenders cap vehicle age at the end of the term. With 45+ lenders there’s a competitive option for most ages of car.
