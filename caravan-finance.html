How does caravan finance work?

Updated 8 July 2026

Caravan finance is a secured loan against the van itself: you apply once, we run your scenario across 45+ lenders, and you get your best options from the panel — usually with same-day pre-approval and no credit score impact at the quote stage. Because the caravan secures the loan, rates generally sit well below unsecured borrowing, even on larger amounts.

New or used, dealer or private sale, from compact camper trailers to full off-road vans and motorhomes — lenders on our panel cover them all. Terms typically run up to 7 years, and deposits or trade-ins are optional for most approved applicants.

Caravans are bigger purchases than most cars, so structuring matters: a balloon payment or a longer term can bring the weekly figure into holiday-budget territory. We model the options and you pick what fits.

How your caravan loan can be shaped.

The structure moves the repayment as much as the rate does. We model the options; you pick what fits.

Secured against the van

The caravan is the security, so rates sit well below personal loans and credit cards — even on six-figure off-road vans.

Terms from 1–7 years

Caravans are long-life assets, so most buyers use longer terms to keep repayments in holiday-budget territory.

Optional balloon payment

A balloon at the end of the term drops the weekly figure along the way — useful on larger vans. Pay it out, refinance or upgrade at the end.

Deposit or trade-in optional

Trade your current van or put savings down to lower the amount financed — or finance the full price if approved.

Caravan finance questions, answered.

The things people ask us every week about caravan finance.

Can I finance a used or privately sold caravan?

Yes. Used vans and private sales are financed every week — the lender adds a PPSR check and pays the seller directly at settlement. Age limits vary by lender, and with 45+ on the panel there’s usually a fit even for older vans.

How much can I borrow for a caravan?

Lenders on our panel fund everything from $5,000 camper trailers to $200,000+ off-road vans and motorhomes. What you can borrow comes down to your income and commitments — the quick quote gives you a realistic figure without touching your credit file.

What loan terms are available for caravan finance?

Commonly 1 to 7 years. Because caravans hold value reasonably well, many buyers pair a longer term with a balloon payment to keep weekly repayments down — we’ll show you both structures so you can compare.

Does a caravan loan quote affect my credit score?

No. The quick quote assesses your scenario against the panel without a credit enquiry. Your file is only touched when you choose a lender and submit a formal application.

Can I include accessories and setup costs in the loan?

Often, yes. Many lenders will fund on-road extras like annexes, solar, batteries and tow gear when they’re part of the purchase. Tell us what you’re quoting on and we’ll match lenders who’ll include it.

Motorhomes and campervans too, or just towed vans?

Both. Motorhomes and campervans are financed the same way — secured against the vehicle. Larger motorhome amounts may involve slightly different lender criteria, which is exactly the sort of thing the panel sorts out for you.

Do I need insurance to finance a caravan?

Yes — lenders require comprehensive insurance on a financed caravan, the same as a financed car. It protects you as much as them; we can point you to insurers who specialise in vans.

Can retirees or part-timers get caravan finance?

Often, yes. Several lenders on the panel assess retirement income, investments and asset position rather than just a payslip. It’s a common scenario for caravan purchases and exactly the kind of placement a broker does well.

What affects my caravan loan rate?

Your credit profile, income and asset position, the van’s age and value, the term, and any deposit. Newer vans and stronger profiles price sharpest — but every lender weighs it differently, so the panel does the comparison work.
