How does commercial finance work?

Updated 8 July 2026

Commercial asset finance — most commonly a chattel mortgage — funds income-producing equipment: the lender advances the purchase price, your business owns the asset from day one, and the asset itself secures the loan. You apply once, 45+ lenders compete, and you get your best options from the panel — with same-day pre-approval and no credit score impact at the quote stage. Low-doc approvals are available for established ABN holders.

The panel funds trucks, trailers, excavators, skid steers, forklifts, agricultural machinery, medical and workshop equipment — new and used, dealer, auction or private sale. Specialist equipment funders often approve assets and ages that banks won’t.

Because the asset works in the business, interest and depreciation are generally deductible, and balloons can be matched to the asset’s working life to protect cash flow. Structuring is where a broker earns their keep — talk to your accountant about the tax side and we’ll structure the finance to match.

Commercial finance questions, answered.

The things people ask us every week about commercial finance.

What is a chattel mortgage?

The standard structure for business asset purchases: the lender funds the asset, your business takes ownership immediately, and the lender registers security over it. Fixed rate, fixed term, optional balloon — and generally deductible interest and depreciation (confirm treatment with your accountant).

Can I get equipment finance with just an ABN — low-doc?

Yes. Established ABN holders (typically 2+ years, GST registered) can access low-doc approvals up to significant limits without full financials, especially on standard assets like trucks and yellow gear.

Do you finance used equipment and auction or private-sale purchases?

Yes — used gear, auction buys and private sales are all fundable. Lenders vary on asset age at end-of-term, which is exactly why running the scenario across the panel beats asking one bank.

Can repayments be matched to seasonal cash flow?

Often, yes. Some lenders offer seasonal or structured repayments for agriculture and other cyclical businesses, and balloons can carry part of the cost to the end of term. Tell us how the cash flows and we’ll structure around it.

Is the temporary full expensing / instant asset write-off available?

Tax incentives for business assets change year to year and depend on your circumstances — your accountant is the right person to confirm what applies. What we do is structure the finance so you can act inside the window once they give the nod.

How fast can commercial finance settle?

Same-day pre-approval is typical; straightforward low-doc deals on standard assets can settle in 24–48 hours. Complex or high-value equipment takes longer — we’ll give you a realistic timeline up front.
