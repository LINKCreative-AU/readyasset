How does leisure finance work?

Updated 8 July 2026

Leisure finance is a secured loan for recreational assets — jet skis, ATVs and quads, camper trailers, horse floats and other toys: you apply once, 45+ lenders compete, and you get your best options from the panel with same-day pre-approval and no credit score impact at the quote stage. Securing the loan against the asset keeps rates below personal-loan territory for most buyers.

New or used, dealer or private sale — the panel covers the lot, including specialist recreational lenders who understand these assets and their resale values. Registered or not, most powered recreational assets can be financed.

Loans typically run 1 to 7 years with optional deposits and balloons. Gear that’s part of the purchase — trailers for the jet ski, accessories for the quad — can often be included in the amount financed.

Leisure finance questions, answered.

The things people ask us every week about leisure finance.

What counts as a leisure asset?

Jet skis and PWCs, ATVs, quads and side-by-sides, dirt bikes, camper trailers, horse floats, box and boat trailers, golf carts — if it makes the weekend better, there’s usually a lender on the panel for it.

Can I finance a jet ski with its trailer?

Yes — ski and trailer are commonly financed together as one package, along with registration and safety gear included in the purchase.

Does a leisure finance quote affect my credit score?

No. The quick quote runs against the panel with no credit enquiry. Your file is only checked when you proceed with a chosen lender.

Are private sales okay for leisure assets?

Yes. The lender verifies the asset and any money owing on it, then pays the seller directly at settlement. It adds a day or so versus a dealer purchase, and we handle the checks.

Is leisure finance dearer than a car loan?

Usually slightly, because recreational assets depreciate differently — but it still prices well below unsecured lending. Your actual rate depends on your profile and the asset; the panel competes and you see your options with no obligation.

Can seasonal workers or self-employed buyers qualify?

Yes — the panel includes lenders comfortable with self-employed, contract and seasonal income. Low-doc style assessment is available for ABN holders with reasonable history.

Does the asset need to be registered?

No — plenty of leisure assets (dirt quads, golf carts, e-bikes) aren’t road-registered and are financed anyway. The lender secures against the asset’s serial/VIN rather than a rego plate.

Is there a minimum loan amount?

Most panel lenders start around $5,000; a few go lower. Below that, a small personal loan usually beats asset finance on fees — we’ll tell you straight which way the maths falls.

What affects my leisure loan rate?

Credit profile, income, the asset type and age, term and deposit. Specialist recreational lenders often beat generalist pricing on toys — the panel finds out in one pass.
