How do personal loans work?

Updated 8 July 2026

A personal loan gives you a lump sum repaid over a fixed term, usually 1 to 7 years: you apply once, we run your scenario across 45+ lenders, and you get your best options from the panel — with same-day pre-approval and no credit score impact at the quote stage. Your rate is personalised to your credit profile and income, which is exactly why comparing one lender is never enough.

Personal loans can be unsecured, or secured against an asset for a lower rate. Common uses are debt consolidation, travel, renovations, medical costs and weddings — anything where a fixed repayment beats an open-ended credit card balance.

Consolidation deserves a special mention: rolling cards and buy-now-pay-later balances into one fixed repayment often reduces both the interest you pay and the monthly juggle. We’ll show the numbers so the decision makes itself.

Personal finance questions, answered.

The things people ask us every week about personal loans.

Will a personal loan quote affect my credit score?

No. The quick quote is a panel assessment, not a credit application — no enquiry is recorded. Your file is only checked once you choose a lender and proceed.

What can I use a personal loan for?

Almost anything legal: consolidating debts, travel, renovations, medical or dental, weddings, furniture, education. Lenders ask the purpose mainly to structure the loan correctly.

Secured or unsecured — which is better?

Secured loans (backed by a car or other asset) generally price lower; unsecured loans need no asset and settle faster. Which wins depends on your situation — the quote shows both where available so you can compare.

How much can I borrow with a personal loan?

Panel lenders typically range from $2,000 to $75,000. Your figure depends on income, commitments and credit history — the quick quote gives you a realistic number with no credit score impact.

Can I consolidate credit cards and BNPL into one loan?

Yes — debt consolidation is one of the most common uses. One fixed repayment at a personalised rate replaces multiple cards and buy-now-pay-later balances, and lenders on our panel compete for exactly this business.

How fast is the money in my account?

Pre-approval is typically same-day. Once you accept an offer and verification is done, many lenders fund within 24–48 hours — some unsecured lenders same-day.

Fixed or variable rate — which should I take?

Most personal loans on the panel are fixed: the repayment never moves, which suits budgeting. Variable products sometimes allow more flexible early payout. We’ll show both where they exist and let the numbers argue it out.

Can I pay my loan out early?

Usually, and many lenders on our panel charge little or nothing for it. If early payout matters to you, say so in the quote — it changes which lenders we shortlist.

What determines my personal loan rate?

Credit score and history, income and job stability, existing commitments, whether the loan is secured, and the term. Because rates are personalised, comparing one lender is meaningless — the panel spread is the real answer.
